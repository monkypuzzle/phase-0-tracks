Who is the walrus, really?

John Lennon once said, "I am the walrus." Since then, many have echoed his words - not least among them, Steve Buscemi in his turn as the Dude's friend Donny. Was John Lennon right? Was he telling the truth? Was he lying? Perhaps we'll never know. But what we do know is this.

  • Walruses have long tusks - really, elongated canines - that serve some unknown purpose
  • Walruses have prominent vibrissae - highly sensitive "whiskers" that probably aid in foraging
  • Walruses are immense, bloated animals with thick blubbery skins

We also know these things about John Lennon:

  1. He was born of human parents - strong evidence suggesting that he was human
  2. He was prone to making figurative statements, saying for example that "all you need is love", when anybody knows that you also need food and shelter and so on
